How every number on this dashboard is calculated

Everything here is built from a few per-SKU quantities, combined two ways. The PVM works in rupees and includes volume (“where did the ₹ go?”). The bridge works in percentage points of GM% and volume cancels out, because a ratio doesn’t depend on scale (“what moved the margin rate?”).

1 · Building blocks (per SKU, per month)

From your file, for each SKU i in a month:

q = adjusted quantity NS = net sales TC = RM + PM + Fo + MFG + Yield Loss (total cost) GM = NS − TC (gross margin) unit price (realisation) p = NS / q unit cost c = TC / q unit margin m = p − c = GM / q

For any group: Q = Σq, GM = ΣGM, and the average unit margin M̄ = GM / Q.

2 · PVM — the ₹ view (Volume / Mix / Price / Cost)

Comparing period 1 (“from”) with period 2 (“to”), the total change ΔGM = GM₂ − GM₁ splits into effects that sum to it exactly:

Volume = (Q₂ − Q₁) · M̄₁ more/fewer units at period-1 avg margin Mix = Σ q₂ᵢ·m₁ᵢ − Q₂·M̄₁ period-2 volumes tilted to high/low-margin SKUs Price = Σ q₂ᵢ·(p₂ᵢ − p₁ᵢ) realisation per unit changed Cost = Σ q₂ᵢ·(c₁ᵢ − c₂ᵢ) unit cost changed (cost down = +) Volume + Mix + Price + Cost + Unallocated = ΔGM
  • Volume holds margin-per-unit fixed at period 1 and asks what the total unit change alone is worth.
  • Mix holds period-1 unit margins fixed and asks whether period-2 volume leaned toward the more (or less) profitable SKUs. Equivalently Σ(q₂ᵢ − Q₂·w₁ᵢ)·m₁ᵢ with w₁ᵢ = q₁ᵢ/Q₁ = period-1 volume share.
  • Price re-prices period-2 volumes by the change in realisation per unit.
  • Cost re-prices period-2 volumes by the change in unit cost (sign flipped so a cost cut reads positive).
  • Unallocated = ΔGM − (the four above); it catches SKUs with zero adjusted qty but non-zero sales (returns-only), where per-unit values don’t exist. Usually tiny.
  • New / Discontinued buckets: when a whole segment (by the “break down by” dimension — e.g. a channel) exists in only one period, its entire margin is shown as a separate New (entered) or Discontinued (left) bar, and Volume/Mix/Price/Cost are computed on the continuing segments only — so entries and exits aren’t smeared into the like-for-like effects. All bars still sum exactly to Δ Gross Margin. Switch the “break down by” selector to regroup them (new products vs new channels vs new parties…).

Note: PVM Price is net realisation (NS/q), so it bundles list-MRP, commission, leakage and GST together. The bridge below pulls those apart.

3 · The bridge — the GM % view

The bridge decomposes the change in the rate GM% = 1 − TC/NS. Realisation per unit is rebuilt as a chain of factors, each defined from your actual totals so the identity is exact:

M = Σ(MRPᵢ·qᵢ)/Q effective list-MRP per unit (write MRP̄ = Σ MRPᵢ·qᵢ) K = (MRP̄ − AdjBill)/MRP̄ commission fraction λ = (AdjBill − Consumer)/AdjBill RTV leakage (expiry + capping) G = Consumer / NS the (1 + GST) factor c = TC / Q effective unit cost p = NS/Q = M · (1−K) · (1−λ) / G (holds exactly) TotalCost% = TC/NS = c·G / [ M·(1−K)·(1−λ) ] GM% = 1 − TotalCost%

Because TotalCost% is a product of those factors, its change splits exactly and additively using LMDI (Logarithmic Mean Divisia Index). The weight is the logarithmic mean of the two TotalCost% values:

Lm = L(TC%₁, TC%₂), where L(a,b) = (b − a) / ln(b/a) (= a if a = b)

That specific weight is what makes the pieces sum to the exact ΔGM% with zero residual. Each lever’s effect on GM% (numerator factors flip sign, since GM% = 1 − TC%):

Cost = − Lm · ln(c₂/c₁) unit cost up → GM% down MRP = + Lm · ln(M₂/M₁) realised MRP up → GM% up Commission = + Lm · ln((1−K₂)/(1−K₁)) more commission → GM% down RTV-leakage = + Lm · ln((1−λ₂)/(1−λ₁)) more expiry/capping → GM% down GST = − Lm · ln(G₂/G₁) higher effective GST → GM% down Cost + MRP + Commission + RTV + GST = ΔGM% ← the “MRP combined” tab

4 · Price vs Mix — splitting the MRP lever

The MRP effect moves whenever the effective MRP per unit M changes — and M is a volume-weighted average of list MRP, so it moves for two different reasons: a real list-price change, or a mix shift toward higher/lower-MRP products even with every price frozen. To separate them, build a fixed-mix (Laspeyres) MRP — period-1 mix, period-2 prices:

M_fix = Σ ( MRPᵢ[period 2] · qᵢ[period 1] ) / Σ qᵢ[period 1] Price = Lm · ln( M_fix / M₁ ) per-SKU list-MRP change only, mix frozen Mix = Lm · ln( M₂ / M_fix ) the leftover — mix shifting Price + Mix = Lm · ln(M₂/M₁) = the whole MRP effect (nothing lost)

This is why Price came out ≥ 0: since no list MRP was cut, M_fix ≥ M₁, so ln(M_fix/M₁) ≥ 0. Any negative on the revenue side (e.g. GT) is therefore forced into Mix — a shift to lower-MRP products — never into Price.

The cross-segment tabs in the Excel use the same construction but with the company as the reference instead of the previous period: Price = the segment’s price level vs company, Mix = the segment’s product mix vs company.

5 · How the two “Prices” relate

  • PVM Price (₹) = change in net realisation per unit — already contains list-price + commission + leakage + GST.
  • Bridge Price (pp) = list-MRP only; commission, RTV and GST are their own levers, and it’s on the rate, so volume is carried separately by the PVM’s Volume line.

6 · Caveats

  • A segment that appears in only one period is tagged New <type> (started in the “to” period) or Discontinued <type> (gone by the “to” period) — e.g. “New Channel”, “New Product”, “New Party”. Only a segment present in both periods whose numbers can’t form a clean bridge (net sales / adjusted qty / billing > 0, sensible ratios) shows n/m.
  • In every breakdown table, each row decomposes its own change, so the ΔGM column sums to the total while Volume/Mix/Price/Cost (or the bridge levers) are computed within that segment — mix across segments differs from mix within them.
  • Gross Margin = Net Sales − RM − PM − Fo − MFG − Yield Loss (matches your channel P&L to the rupee). All computation runs locally in your browser.
